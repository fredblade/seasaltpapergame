**Sea Salt & Paper – Full Rules Summary** (Base Game)

**Players**: 2–4  
**Playtime**: 30–45 minutes  
**Ages**: 8+  
**Goal**: Be the first to reach the target score (40 points for 2 players, 35 for 3 players, 30 for 4 players) over multiple rounds.

### Components
- 58 cards (including various sea creatures, duos, collectors, mermaids, etc.)
- 6 player aids (reference sheets)

### Setup
1. Give each player a player aid.
2. Shuffle the deck and place it face down within reach of all players.
3. Reveal the top two cards from the deck and place one on each of the **two discard piles** (these piles are open and visible to everyone).
4. Determine the starting player (e.g., youngest or random).

### Card Types (Overview)
Cards generally fall into these categories:
- **Numbered/colored cards** for sets and points.
- **Duo cards** (pairs you play for special effects): Crab, Boat, Fish, etc.
- **Collector cards** (e.g., Octopus, Shell, Penguin, Sailor) — score based on how many matching smaller cards you have.
- **Mermaids** (special scoring).
- Other unique cards like Lighthouse, Shoal of Fish, Penguin Colony, Captain, etc.

Exact point values and effects are printed on the cards and player aids.

### Gameplay (A Round)
Players take turns clockwise. On your turn, you perform actions in this order:

1. **Draw** (mandatory):
   - **Option A**: Draw the top **two cards** from the deck. Keep **one** in your hand, and place the other on **one of the two discard piles** (face up). If one discard pile is empty, you must discard to it.
   - **Option B**: Take the **top card** from one of the two discard piles and add it to your hand.

2. **Duo** (optional):
   - If you have a matching **pair (duo)** of the same duo card type in your hand, you may play them face up in front of you to trigger their effect. You can play multiple pairs if you have them.
   - Common Duo effects:
     - **Crab**: Take one card of your choice from either discard pile.
     - **Boat**: Take another full turn immediately.
     - **Fish**: Draw the top card from the deck and add it to your hand (no discard).

3. **End the Round?** (optional, but only if you have **7 or more points** in your current hand + played cards):
   - You may declare **STOP** — the round ends immediately. Everyone reveals their hands and scores.
   - Or declare **LAST CHANCE** — everyone else gets one final turn. After that, compare scores (see Scoring below).

You may play duos before deciding to end the round.

### Ending a Round & Scoring
At the end of a round (via STOP or after LAST CHANCE), every player scores their hand + played cards:

- **Base points** from all your cards (hand + table).
- **Mermaids** have special scoring (usually strong points; collecting all 4 mermaids in one round wins the game instantly).
- **Collector sets**: Score based on how many matching cards you collected (e.g., for Octopus, Shells, etc.).
- **Color bonus** (only in certain cases — see below).

**If someone called STOP**:
- All players score their full points.

**If someone called LAST CHANCE**:
- Compare final scores.
- If the caller has the **strictly highest** score: They get their points **+ color bonus** (1 extra point per card of their most numerous color). Other players get **only** their color bonus.
- If any other player ties or beats the caller: Everyone (including the caller) scores their base points, but the caller gets **only** their color bonus.

### Game End
Play rounds until one player reaches or exceeds the target score (40/35/30). Highest score wins. Ties are usually broken by further play or agreed method.

### Key Notes & Clarifications
- You score **all** your cards (in hand and played on table).
- You can only call the end of the round if you have **at least 7 points**.
- Discard piles are public — you can look at the top cards but not dig through them.
- The game has light push-your-luck and risk/reward elements around when to stop vs. give others a chance.
- Mermaids are powerful and can swing scoring significantly.

These are the core base game rules. Expansions like *Extra Salt* or *Extra Pepper* add new cards, multipliers, events, etc.
